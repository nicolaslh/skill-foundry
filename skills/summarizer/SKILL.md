# Summarizer

## Overview

Produce faithful summaries that distinguish source facts from interpretation. Keep the output compact, useful, and shaped to the user's requested audience or decision.

## Workflow

1. Identify the source type, audience, desired length, and output format.
2. Extract the central thesis, major evidence, decisions, action items, risks, and unresolved questions.
3. Preserve numbers, names, dates, and caveats exactly when they matter.
4. Separate "what the source says" from analysis or recommendations.
5. Note gaps when the source is missing enough context for a confident summary.

## Output Formats

- Executive brief: one paragraph plus bullets.
- Meeting summary: decisions, action items, owners, open questions.
- Article summary: thesis, key points, implications, caveats.
- Research digest: findings, evidence quality, contradictions, next checks.

## Output Checklist

- Concise and source-grounded
- No invented facts
- Key caveats preserved
- Actionable structure for the user's goal
