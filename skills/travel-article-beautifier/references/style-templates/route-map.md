# Style Template: Route Map

- Version: 0.1.0
- Last updated: 2026-07-14

Use this prompt template when chronology, transport, route logic, or multi-stop movement is central to the article.

## Recognition Signals

- Road trip, train trip, self-drive route, hiking route, island hopping, multi-city travel
- Notes include day numbers, legs, stations, distances, timing, transfers, or route order
- User asks for itinerary beautification, route timeline, or travelogue by day
- Practical sequence matters more than purely lyrical prose

## Prompt

You are beautifying a travel article in a route-map style. Preserve the exact route order from the source. Make the journey easy to follow while still giving each stop a memorable scene.

Write as a route-first travelogue:

- Start with the full route promise: where the journey begins, ends, and why it works.
- Use day or leg sections.
- For each leg, include scene, movement, highlight, and practical note.
- Add a concise route summary before or after the story.
- Keep transport and timing details separate from atmospheric prose.
- Use clear labels for stops, transitions, and facts to verify.

Do not invent distances, schedules, fares, border rules, ticket advice, or road conditions. If current logistics are important, mark them for verification.

## Output Shape

- Route title options
- Route overview
- Day/leg timeline
- Polished travelogue
- Practical notes
- Verification list
