# Source Intake

Use this reference when turning scattered travel material into a usable article brief.

## Required Inputs

- Destination and route
- Trip date or season
- Travel companions or solo context
- Trip duration
- Source material: notes, diary fragments, photos, captions, maps, tickets, restaurant lists, or existing draft
- Target reader and platform
- Desired output: Markdown, HTML, social post, or mixed package

## Brief Shape

Create a compact brief before drafting:

```markdown
# Article Brief

## Promise
What the reader will get from this article.

## Audience
Who this is for and what they care about.

## Route
Chronological route or thematic route.

## Strongest Scenes
Specific moments worth expanding.

## Practical Facts
Logistics provided by the user and facts needing verification.

## Tone
Calm, literary, useful, playful, luxurious, minimalist, family-friendly, backpacker, etc.

## Output
Markdown, HTML, or both.
```

## Fact Handling

Never convert guesswork into certainty. If the source says "maybe", "around", "I think", or lacks dates, preserve that uncertainty or ask for confirmation when it matters.
